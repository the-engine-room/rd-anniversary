Responsible Data turns 5!

Celebrating 5 years of leading the way from best intentions to best practice

Responsible Data’s anniversary retrospective

Five years ago, a small group of practitioners wanting to address the challenges and opportunities for responsibly using data for social change came together as a budding Responsible Data community. Today, we celebrate the hundreds of community members who contribute to this space with healthy debates and resource-sharing about strategy, compliance, innovation and emerging risks!

Community Stories

Paola Verhaert

Knowledge Coordinator at The Engine Room “My favourite thing is to watch teams adopt RD language and practices as their own.”
X

How did you become a part of the Responsible Data community?

I have a background as a historian, but I was also learning the ins and outs of data science and programming as part of my degree in Digital Humanities. In the conversative “tech community,” I was frustrated with practices on data-sharing, privacy and handling data and information in an ethical and respectful way, and I was disappointed about the lack of attention these topics received. Then, in 2016, I joined The Engine Room, who are stewards of the RD community. I immediately felt like I had found the people I was looking for, and I can’t begin to count the number of interesting conversations about RD I’ve had since!

What event from the last five years stuck with you the most?

It’s been amazing to witness organisations deciding to prioritise RD as a practice and implementing a policy or guidelines in their own way. I always enjoy working on RD in a hands-on way, and I’m lucky to have supported in the development of RD policies for a number of organisations. My favourite thing is to watch teams adopt RD language and practices as their own.

Looking ahead, which emerging topics related to RD are following and thinking about?

The world is not the same place it was in 2014. In the European Union, for example, we saw the implementation of the GDPR in May 2018. It’s still so new, and I’m curious to see not only how different member states implement the regulation, but also to see how other countries and regions take inspiration from this piece of legislation. It may not be perfect, but it catalysed the conversation in ways that haven’t become visible yet.
